TAKE ME HOME

WHY DO YOU CONSIDER YOURSELF MORE OF A PERFORMER THAN A DRAG QUEEN ?
I think it’s just because this has been suggested to me so many times in the past from different audiences, that I kind of took that way of looking at it after a while. I’ve never embraced show tunes for example and never been one to get excited by the fashion that’s involved with drag. I have always removed my wig at the end of each show also - it was and still isn’t intentional - it just feels natural to do so to show that audience at the end of each performance that I am just a bloke whom happens to be dressed up in ladies clothes and having fun making people laugh.

Come on over

HAVE YOU NOTICED A CHANGE IN THE AUDIENCES YOU PERFOM TO SINCE YOU STARTED YOUR ACT ?
Completely. The attention span of people these days is ludicrously short, which it turn makes the show we do on stage that much more of a challenge. Back in the day ( sounding old now ) the timings of cabaret were much earlier and people didn’t have their phones as distractions - they came to see the cabaret and to be taken someone else for an hour or so. Now however, it’s a fight to get peoples attention at the best of times, and the show has to be a great deal more paced than back say 10-15 years ago. It’s still great fun - just more of a challenge.

IS THERE A SEPARATION BETWEEN YOU AND WHO YOU PERFORM AS?
There is and there isn’t. When I take the make up off, I am quite a shy person. I am not one of those people on my day off that would get up on stage and perform if pulled up by a fellow artist for example. That said, the humour and energy you see on stage is exactly what I am like off stage too - I don’t rest much - always causing trouble and trying to spread some laughter wherever I can. That may sound quite contradictory, but hope it makes some sense.
