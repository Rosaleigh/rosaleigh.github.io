TAKE ME HOME

Outfits fit for landfills

WHY DO YOU CONSIDER YOURSELF MORE OF A PERFORMER THAN A DRAG QUEEN?
My main drag influences include non drags such as Judy (as in Richard &), Anna Nicole Smith, Mariah Carey etc. I think the common element I find inspiring is these are all people who do not let a lack of discernible talent stand between them and their dreams, and I aspire to be at their level. As for drag queens that have influenced my performance style, Christeene would probably be the biggest. And just for good measure, my favourite girl from drag race is Tammie Brown because I can’t believe they let her on TV and she’s a truly unique performer, and honourable mention to Mrs Kasha Davis who actually put on an amazing show at bar Broadway two years ago.

WHY DID YOU START DOING DRAG ?
I’ve always loved dressing up in wigs and heels, and then once I got to uni there was plenty of opportunity to do so quite regularly. Eventually I tried performing and I was completely shit in the most boring way because I didn’t really have anything to say. Nearly two years later and I’m still totally shit but for all the right reasons. I took my makeup from vaguely pretty to totally garish, only wear outfits fit for landfills, and my performances feel as though they have been produced by an algorithm or some sort of drag fruit machine (last night the style was country music, the topic was masturbation and the outfit was made out of waste paper from taco bell). Somehow it all works and this is how and why I continue with drag even if I can’t quite pinpoint exactly why it started. I first started properly performing in drag I guess when I got to a point where just conventional outrage at injustice and systematic oppression wasn’t really cutting it for me anymore. Every number I create now is just one more tiny, basically ineffective, fuck you to the lizards. It keeps me going.

DO YOU GO OUT IN FULL DRAG ANY OTHER TIME THAN TO PERFORM ?
I love getting into drag for any occasion, not just performing. Themed parties are the biggest opportunity, if I’m dressing up it had better be drag, boys clothes literally all look the same. If gender is a performance then dragging up at every opportunity is my way to avoid being typecast.

AT WHAT POINT DO YOU BECOME YOUR DRAG CHARACTER ?
I don’t think I undergo I huge attitude adjustment when I get into drag, but I think maybe I am less afraid to totally be myself. However as far is the physical transformation goes, I am in drag as soon as the big triangle eyes are done. They’re my trademark, I invented them as a way of getting around a lack of any make up ability and now I’d never change them, people actually recognise me by them because it’s about the only consistent element of my drag.
